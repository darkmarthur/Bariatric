WHAT IS OBESITY?

Currently, it is accepted that anyone with a Body Mass Index (BMI) greater than 30 suffers obesity and if this index is 40 or higher, suffers morbid obesity. The BMI is the relationship between what we weigh and what we measure and can be easily obtained- To the extent that the BMI and overweight increase, we talk about Obesity (BMI> 30), Morbid Obesity (BMI> 40), Super-Morbid (BMI> 50), Super-Super-Morbid (BMI> 60) and so on. Currently obesity is a serious worldwide health problem, this is the cause of many associated diseases that leads to deterioration in the quality of life and increases the risk of prematurely dying.

DISEASES ASSOCIATED WITH OBESITY

  • Diabetes mellitus type 2
  • Arterial hypertension
  • High cholesterol or triglycerides (hyperlipidemia)
  • Sleep apnea
  • Fatty liver
  • Cardiovascular diseases
  • Embolias
  • Various types of cancer including uterine, cervical, ovarian, breast, colon, rectum and prostate.
  • Infertility and Irregular Menstrual Cycles
  • Depression
  • Joint problems
  • Metabolic syndrome
  • Varicose veins
  • Headache
  • Gastroesophageal reflux.

THE OBESITY DECREASES THE QUALITY OF LIFE OF IMPORTANT WAYS, ARRIVING TO HAVE DIFFICULTY FOR EVERYDAY ACTIVITIES LIKE TO DRESS, TO BATH, TO WALK. THEREFORE THEY STOP DOING THINGS THAT WE ENJOYED, THE PLEASURE TO BE ALLOWED TO HAVE A BETTER SOCIAL LIFE, SUCH AS STROLLING, SWIMMING, RUNNING, RIDING ON A BIKE, OR PLAYING WITH OUR CHILDREN. YOU CAN´T ALSO TRAVEL COMFORTABLE AND SAFE. A LOWER QUALITY OF LIFE INCREASES THE DEPRESSION INDEX ENTERING IN A VICIOUS CIRCLE FROM WHICH IT COMES VERY DIFFICULT TO LEAVE.

MORBID OBESITY HAS FOUR DEFINING CHARACTERISTICS:

Metabolic disease

It is a complex disease and must be treated and considered as such. When it appears or develops it does so progressively and uncontrollably, not depending at all on the patient's will to achieve a normal weight and only a specialist in the field can help these patients.


SERIOUS ILLNESS

Because it is accompanied by multiple co-morbidities that do not heal unless obesity is resolved. We can highlight some as Type 2 diabetes, respiratory failure, hypertension, sleep apnea, arthropathy, dyslipidemia, heart disease, depression and others without counting the psycho-social conditions. It predisposes to the appearance of cancer, considerably shortens life prospects, is very disabling and causes social rejection and isolation.


CHRONIC DISEASE

That is to say "for life".
When medical and dietary treatments have failed, only surgery can help a satisfactory weight loss, maintain it over time and correct many of the co-morbidities.


UNCONTROLLABLE DISEASE

It does not depend "on the strength of the will" of the patient, but rather it is the disease itself that dominates the patient's mind and body. Today we know that human fat tissue is able to make a whole series of hormones that make it function as an endocrine organ more. These hormones act by modulating and regulating the impulses of hunger and satiety at the level of the hypothalamus in our brain.
