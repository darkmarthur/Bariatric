Understand My Surgery

The surgery of obesity has not been designed to "cure" sick obeses, but yes to be able to control it for life.

Calculate BMI Schedule Now

Undoubtedly, you understood the explanations that we gave you before operating on the type of intervention to be performed. Now you must assimilate that your stomach has been sectioned almost completely and that the useful space that will be filled with food is very small. Also the hole through which the food will pass to the rest of the digestive tract is very narrow, so that with very little liquid or soft foods can feel "full". If you continue to eat you may have a painful sensation of abdominal pressure, nausea and vomiting.

Both you and your family should understand that the surgery is designed to cause a drastic food restriction, which will lead to the disappearance of excess weight accumulated in the form of fat. Many associated diseases that lead to a deterioration in the quality of life and significantly increases the risk of dying prematurely.

In general, the feeling of hunger or desire to eat will disappear after surgery with very little fluid or food. Today we know that the control of the hormone called ghrelin (appetite stimulant), which occurs in the stomach and is largely deactivated with surgery, along with other hormonal effects that act by increasing satiety at the hypothalamic level, are decisive in the process of weight loss, although this may change over time, so that food reeducation and behavioral habits must be maintained indefinitely, throughout life.

Obesity Surgery has not been designed to "cure" patients who suffer from it, but to control it for life. It has been put into operation as the only valid alternative in medical practice to achieve an acceptable weight loss and a reduction or disappearance of diseases associated with obesity such as diabetes, hypertension, heart disease, respiratory failure, infertility, depression, apnea sleep, arthropathy and others. Surgeons specialized in these techniques put "traps" to the body to avoid excessive intake of food that leads to obesity and introduce mechanisms that trigger hormonal effects some known and others in the research phase and that lead to the recovery of health and to the control of the morbid status.

Therefore, the patient's and family's understanding of their particular "trap" and their collaboration with our recommendations are vital for the desired effects to be maintained throughout life. Attempting to forcefully eat larger quantities of food will cause vomiting and retrosternal pain, due to excessive distension of your small gastric reservoir. Being eating all day long, even if they are small amounts, means a lot of extra calories. Eating or drinking excessively sugary foods or foods rich in animal and hypercaloric fats, such as ice cream, cream, chocolates, breads, nuts, alcoholic beverages or Coca-Cola type, can make the "traps" do not work and you are cheating your own Surgical technique. In short, you can make the most of your surgery and maintain it throughout your life, but you must be willing to follow our instructions and control the type of food and how to eat.
